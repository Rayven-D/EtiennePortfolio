FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT APP (MOBILE) Designing a budgeting experience that prioritizes clarity, confidence, and approachable financial guidance.
PROJECT_OVERVIEW A budgeting app designed to help students manage their finances without feeling overwhelmed. The challenge was making money management feel achievable rather than intimidating.

By focusing on clarity and supportive visual feedback, the app turns complex financial data into simple, digestible insights that encourage ongoing engagement.
TARGET_AUDIENCE Designed for students managing personal finances for the first time.
  • Students learning to budget and track spending
  • Users who want simple, visual financial feedback
  • Individuals who may feel overwhelmed by traditional banking apps
  • Users looking for quick insights into spending habits
01
PHASE_01: RESEARCH [RESEARCH & DISCOVERY]
We explored how students interact with budgeting tools and where traditional apps fall short. Research revealed that financial anxiety is a significant barrier. People avoid budgeting apps because they fear bad news. The goal became creating an experience that felt supportive rather than judgmental. Key research activities:
  • Competitive analysis of budgeting and fintech apps
  • Research on student financial behaviors and pain points
  • Analysis of how financial data visualization affects user emotion
02
PHASE_02: VISUAL DIRECTION [BRANDING & CONCEPT EXPLORATION]
With research insights, we developed concepts and built the visual identity. The objective was a friendly, approachable aesthetic that felt less like a bank and more like a helpful companion. I focused on:
  • Sketching layout concepts and early ideas
  • Development of branding (color, typography, tone)
  • Exploration of a friendly and non-intimidating visual language
03
PHASE_03: SYSTEM DEVELOPMENT [UI DESIGN, PROTOTYPING & DESIGN SYSTEM]
We translated concepts into high-fidelity designs and built a cohesive design system. This included creating reusable components, establishing visual patterns, and prototyping key user flows to ensure consistency and scalability. The system included:
  • High-fidelity UI for core screens
  • Interactive prototypes for key flows
  • Reusable components (buttons, cards, progress indicators)
  • Typography, spacing, and color guidelines
  • Consistent patterns for budgeting and data visualization
04
PHASE_04: VALIDATION [TESTING & ITERATION]
Testing revealed how users interpreted the budget visuals and where confusion occurred. We refined the hierarchy, adjusted interaction patterns, and iterated based on feedback to ensure the experience felt clear and supportive. Testing priorities:
  • Usability testing on budget visual comprehension
  • Evaluation of how quickly users interpret spending status
  • Iteration on hierarchy and interaction patterns based on feedback
05
PHASE_05: DELIVERY [FINAL DESIGN]
Tested designs to evaluate usability, clarity, and emotional tone, ensuring the experience felt supportive rather than overwhelming.
  • Usability testing on understanding budget visuals
  • Evaluation of how quickly users interpret spending status
  • Identification of friction in navigation and layout
  • Iteration on hierarchy and interaction patterns
  • Refinement based on team and user feedback
