UX/UI DESIGN - 2025
STREAMING PLATFORM UX Designing a cross-platform streaming experience with a focus on intuitive navigation and interaction across TV and mobile.
PROJECT_OVERVIEW Designed a cross-platform streaming experience using Reddit's brand system for both TV and mobile.
Focused on creating intuitive navigation and clear interaction across different input types, with special attention to remote-based navigation on TV. The goal was to make content browsing and discovery simple, consistent, and easy to use across devices.
TARGET_AUDIENCE Designed for users who browse and watch content across TV and mobile.
  • Users who want simple, intuitive navigation
  • Viewers using remote controls (TV) and touch (mobile)
  • People familiar with content-heavy platforms like Reddit or streaming apps
01
PHASE_01: RESEARCH [RESEARCH & DISCOVERY]
I started by diving into Reddit's brand guidelines and studying how major streaming platforms like Netflix and Apple TV handle navigation. The goal was understanding how people browse content on TV versus mobile, and why remote-based navigation feels so different from touch Key research activities:
  • Analysis of Reddit's visual identity and tone
  • Study of TV interface patterns from Netflix, Apple TV, and others
  • Identification of key pain points in remote-based navigation
02
PHASE_02: EARLY DESIGN [IDEATION & WIREFRAMING]
Armed with research insights, I moved into exploration mode. I sketched different layout structures and tested how content could flow across platforms. The big question? Should navigation be horizontal or vertical? And what patterns could actually translate between TV and mobile? I focused on:
  • Sketching multiple navigation approaches
  • Building low-fidelity wireframes for both platforms
  • Testing horizontal vs. vertical browsing patterns
03
PHASE_03: INTERACTIVE COMPONENTS [INTERACTION & PROTOTYPING]
This is where things got real. I built interactive prototypes with working focus states for TV and touch interactions for mobile. Every button, every transition needed to feel responsive and clear, especially on TV where users rely entirely on visual feedback. The prototype included:
  • Interactive components with motion design
  • Focus state system for remote navigation
  • Core user flows across both platforms
04
PHASE_04: FINAL TV DESIGN [REMOTE-BASED NAVIGATION]
The TV experience needed bold focus states and simplified hierarchy. Viewing from 10 feet away is completely different from holding a phone. Everything had to be larger, clearer, and easier to navigate with just four directional buttons. Design priorities:
  • Horizontal browsing optimized for D-pad controls
  • Strong visual feedback for focus states
  • Large typography designed for distance viewing
05
PHASE_05: FINAL MOBILE DESIGN [TOUCH INTERACTION]
Mobile was all about speed and accessibility. Vertical scrolling, thumb-friendly zones, and streamlined navigation. Everything optimized for quick, on-the-go browsing. Mobile optimizations:
  • Vertical scrolling with thumb-friendly layout
  • Faster access to content with simplified steps
  • Touch gestures replacing directional controls
PROJECT REFLECTION [OUTCOME & LEARNINGS]
Outcome

The final designs were delivered as a comprehensive Figma prototype with full interaction specs for both platforms. It became a proof-of-concept for how Reddit could expand into streaming, showing that their brand could adapt to entirely new contexts. Key Learnings
  • States are everything on TV. What works on mobile doesn't translate. Users need constant visual feedback
  • Simpler is better for TV, even if it means showing less content per screen
  • Horizontal navigation won out for TV after testing showed it felt more natural with D-pad controls
  • Platform constraints aren't limitations. They're design opportunities
